Resposta 1.

Resposta 2.
\(\frac{-1}{x}\)
Resposta 3.
\(\frac{6}{x}\)
Resposta 4.
\(\frac{2x}{x^{2}+1}+\frac{\sin\!\left(x\right)}{2+\cos\!\left(x\right)}\)
Solução.
Solução:
Use o Tereoma fundamental do Cálculo (Part 1).
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{d}{dx} \int_{22}^x \dfrac{1}{t} \, dt = \dfrac{1}{x}. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{d}{dx} \int_{x}^{2} \dfrac{1}{t} \, dt = -\dfrac{d}{dx} \int_{2}^x \dfrac{1}{t} \, dt = -\dfrac{1}{x}. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{d}{dx} \int_{16}^{x^{6}} \dfrac{1}{t} \, dt = \dfrac{1}{x^{6}} \cdot (x^{6})' = \dfrac{1}{x^{6}} \cdot 6 x^{5} = \dfrac{6}{x}. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} \displaystyle \dfrac{d}{dx} \int_{2 + \cos x}^{x^2 + 1} \dfrac{1}{t} \, dt \amp =\amp \displaystyle \dfrac{1}{x^2 + 1} \cdot (x^2 + 1)' - \dfrac{1}{2 + \cos x} \cdot (2 + \cos x)' \\ \amp =\amp \dfrac{2x}{x^2 + 1} - \dfrac{-\sin x}{2 + \cos x} \end{array} \end{equation*}
