Solução 1.37.1.

Fazendo \(u=\ln{x}\text{,}\) obtemos \(\frac{\dd u}{\dd x} = 1/x\text{.}\) Então \(\dd u = (1/x)\dd x\text{.}\) Quando \(x=1\text{,}\) \(u=\ln{1}=0\text{,}\) e, quando \(x=e\) , \(u=\ln{e}=1\text{.}\) Assim,
\begin{align*} \integrald{\frac{\ln{x}}{x}}{1}{e}{x}\amp = \integrald{u}{0}{1}{u} = \frac{u^2}{2}\Biggr\rvert_0^{1} \\ \amp = \left(\frac{1^2}{2} -\frac{0^2}{2}\right)=\frac{1}{2} \text{.} \end{align*}
