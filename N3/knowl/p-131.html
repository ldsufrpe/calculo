Escolha \(u=\tan{t}\text{.}\) Então, \(\frac{\dd u}{\dd t}=(1/\cos^2{t})\) e \(\dd u= (1/\cos^2{t})\dd t\text{.}\) Quando \(t=0\text{,}\) \(u=\tan{0}=0\text{,}\) e, quando, \(x=\pi/4\text{,}\) \(u=\tan{\pi/4}=1\text{.}\) Portanto,
\begin{align*} \integrald{\frac{\tan{t}}{\cos^2{t}}}{0}{\frac{\pi}{4}}{t}\amp = \integrald{\tan{t}\cdot\left(\frac{1}{\cos^2{t}}\right)}{0}{\frac{\pi}{4}}{t}\\ \amp = \integrald{u}{0}{1}{u} = \frac{u^2}{2} \Biggr\rvert_0^{1} = \left(\frac{1^2}{2}-\frac{0^2}{2}\right) \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2} \text{.} \end{align*}
