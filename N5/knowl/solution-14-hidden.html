Integramos a subtração da curva inferior da curva superior de \(-\pi/4\) até \(\pi/4\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{-\pi/4}^{\pi/4} 5^2 \sec^2(x) \amp - 9 \cos(x) \; dx = 5^2 \int_{-\pi/4}^{\pi/4} \sec^2(x) \; dx - 9 \int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/4} \cos(x) \; dx \\\\ \amp = \left. 5^2 \tan(x) \right|_{-\pi/4}^{\pi/4} - \left. 9 \sin(x) \right|_{-\pi/4}^{\pi/4} \\\\ \amp = 5^2 \left( \tan(\pi/4) - \tan(-\pi/4) \right) - 9 \left( \sin(\pi/4) - \sin(-\pi/4) \right) \\\\ \amp = 5^2 \left( 1 - (-1) \right) - 9 \left( \frac{\sqrt[]{2}}{2} - \left( - \frac{\sqrt[]{2}}{2} \right) \right) \\\\ \amp = 50 - 9 \sqrt[]{2} = 37.2720779386421 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
