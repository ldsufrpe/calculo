Solução 1.5.1.

Já que \(a=1\text{,}\) \(b=2\) e \(n=5\) temos \(\Delta x = \frac{2-1}{5}=\frac{1}{5}\text{.}\) Dessa forma, os subintervalos são

\begin{equation*} [1, \tfrac{6}{5}], \, [\tfrac{6}{5}, \tfrac{7}{5}], \, [\tfrac{7}{5}, \tfrac{8}{5}], \, [\tfrac{8}{5}, \tfrac{9}{5}], \, [\tfrac{9}{5}, 2]. \end{equation*}

Logo, \(x_0=1, \, x_1= \tfrac{6}{5}, \, x_2=\tfrac{7}{5}, x_3=\tfrac{8}{5}, \, x_4=\tfrac{9}{5},\, x_5=2\text{.}\) Portanto, de acordo com a Regra do Trapézio:

\begin{align*} \integrald{\frac{1}{x}}{1}{2}{x} \amp \approx \frac{1}{2}\cdot \frac{1}{5}\left[ f(1) + 2f\left(\tfrac{6}{5}\right) + 2f\left(\tfrac{7}{5}\right) + 2f\left(\tfrac{8}{5}\right) + 2f\left(\tfrac{9}{5}\right)+ f(2)\right]\amp \\ \amp = \frac{1}{10}\left[1 + 2\cdot \frac{5}{6} + 2\cdot\frac{5}{7} + 2 \cdot\frac{5}{8}+ 2\cdot\frac{5}{9} + \frac{1}{2}\right]\\ \amp \approx 0.695634 \end{align*}
