Exercício 2.

Use a Regra de Simpson para aproximar \(\integrald{\frac{1}{1+ x^4}}{0}{2}{x}\) usando \(n=4\) .
Resposta.
\(\approx 1,080553 \)
Solução.
Para \(n=4\text{,}\) \(a=0\) e \(b=2\) temos \(x_0=0, \, x_1=\tfrac{1}{2}, \, x_2=1,\, x_3=\tfrac{3}{2},\, x_4=2\text{.}\) Logo, pela Regra de Simpson:
\begin{align*} \integrald{\frac{1}{1+ x^4}}{0}{2}{x} \approx \amp \frac{1}{6}\left[f(0) + 4 f(\tfrac{1}{2}) + 2f(1) + 4f(\tfrac{3}{2}) + f(2)\right]\\ \amp \approx \frac{1}{6}\left[1 + 4\cdot \frac{16}{17} + 2\cdot\frac{1}{2} + 4\cdt\frac{16}{97} + \frac{1}{17}\right]\\ \amp \approx 1,080553 \end{align*}
