Exemplo 1.3.

Use a Regra do Trapézio para calcular \(\integrald{x^2}{0}{1}{x}\) usando 4 subintervalos.
Solução.
Já que \(a=0\text{,}\) \(b=0\) e \(n=4\) temos \(\Delta x = \frac{1-0}{4}=\frac{1}{4}\text{.}\) Nesse caso, os subintervalos são
\begin{align*} \left[0, \tfrac{1}{4}\right],\, \left[\tfrac{1}{4},\, \tfrac{1}{2}\right],\, \left[\tfrac{1}{2}, \tfrac{3}{4}\right], \left[\tfrac{3}{4}, 1\right] \amp \text{.} \end{align*}
Daí, \(x_0=0, \, x_1=\frac{1}{4}, \, x_2=\frac{1}{2},\, x_3= \frac{3}{4}, \, x_4=1\text{.}\) Portanto, de acordo com a Regra do Trapézio:
\begin{align*} \integrald{x^2}{0}{1}{x} \amp \approx \frac{1}{2}\cdot \frac{1}{4}\left[ f(0) + 2f\left(\tfrac{1}{4}\right) + 2f\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\right) + 2f\left(\tfrac{3}{4}\right)+ f(1)\right]\amp \\ \amp =\frac{1}{8}\left[0 + 2\cdot \frac{1}{16} + 2\cdot \frac{1}{4} + 2\cdot \frac{9}{16} + 1\right] \\ \amp = \frac{11}{32} \\ \amp \approx 0.34375 \end{align*}
