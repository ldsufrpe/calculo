Solução 1.10.1.

Do Exemplo 1.3 temos que \(x_0=0, \, x_1=\frac{1}{4}, \, x_2=\frac{1}{2},\, x_3= \frac{3}{4}, \, x_4=1\text{.}\) Portanto, de acordo com a Regra de Simpson:

\begin{align*} \integrald{x^2}{0}{1}{x} \amp \approx \frac{1}{3}\cdot \frac{1}{4}\left[ f(0) + 4f\left(\tfrac{1}{4}\right) + 2f\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\right) + 4f\left(\tfrac{3}{4}\right)+ f(1)\right]\amp \\ \amp =\frac{1}{12}\left[0 + 4\cdot \frac{1}{16} + 2\cdot \frac{1}{4} + 4\cdot \frac{9}{16} + 1\right] \\ \amp = \frac{1}{3} \\ \amp \approx 0.333333 \end{align*}
