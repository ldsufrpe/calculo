Solução 1.5.10.1.

Escrevendo

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{(x+6)(x+8)}=\frac{A}{x+6}+\frac{B}{x+8}, \end{equation*}

temos

\begin{equation*} 1 = A(x+8)+B(x+6), \end{equation*}

ou

\begin{equation*} 1 = (A+B)x+ (8 A+6 B). \end{equation*}

Resolvendo para \(A\) e \(B\text{,}\) encotramos \(A = 0.5\) e \(B = -0.5\text{,}\) então

\begin{equation*} \int \frac{1}{(x+6)(x+8)}\,dx= \int\frac{0.5}{x+6}\,dx + \int \frac{-0.5}{x+8}\,dx = (0.5*ln(|x+6|)-0.5*ln(|x+8|)+C). \end{equation*}
