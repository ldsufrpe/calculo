Usando frações parciais,

\begin{equation*} \frac{3x+5}{\left(x-1\right)\!\left(x+7\right)}=\frac{A}{x-1}+\frac{B}{x+7} \end{equation*}

temos

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} A+B=3\\ 7A-B=5 \end{cases} \end{equation*}

produzindo

\begin{equation*} A=1,\;B=2 . \end{equation*}

Logo,

\begin{equation*} \int \frac{3x+5}{x^{2}+6x-7} \;dx= \int\left( \frac{1}{x-1}+\frac{2}{x+7}\right) \;dx=\ln\!\left(\left|x-1\right|\right)+2\ln\!\left(\left|x+7\right|\right)+C=\ln\!\left(\left|\left(x-1\right)\!\left(x+7\right)^{2}\right|\right)+C . \end{equation*}
