Solução 1.5.9.1.

Já que \(49 - x^2 =(7-x)(7+x)\text{,}\) temos

\begin{equation*} \frac{56}{49 - x^2}= \frac{A}{7-x} +\frac{B}{7+x}. \end{equation*}

Então,

\begin{equation*} 56 = A(7 + x)+B(7 -x) \end{equation*}

e

\begin{equation*} 56 =(A-B)x +5A+5B, \end{equation*}

de modo que

\begin{equation*} A-B = 0 \end{equation*}

e

\begin{equation*} 7 A+ B = 56. \end{equation*}

Assim \(A=B=4\) e

\begin{equation*} \frac{56}{49-x^2}=\frac{4}{7-x} +\frac{4}{7+x}. \end{equation*}
