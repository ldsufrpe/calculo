nOTE QUE \(x^2 + 10 x + 21 = (x + 7)(x + 3)\text{.}\) Assim, escrevemos
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{(x+7)(x+3)}=\frac{A}{x+7}+\frac{B}{x+3}, \end{equation*}
gerando
\begin{equation*} 1 = A(x+3)+B(x+7), \end{equation*}
ou
\begin{equation*} 1 = (A+B)x+ (3 A+7 B). \end{equation*}
Resolvendo para \(A\) e \(B\text{,}\) encontramos \(A = -0.25\) e \(B = 0.25\text{,}\) então
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{1}{(x+7)(x+3)}\,dx= \int\frac{-0.25}{x+7}\,dx + \int \frac{0.25}{x+3}\,dx = 0.25\ln\!\left(\left|x+3\right|\right)-0.25\ln\!\left(\left|x+7\right|\right)+C. \end{equation*}
