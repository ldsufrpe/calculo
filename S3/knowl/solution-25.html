Solução 1.82.1.

If \(u = 14-9x\) then \(du = -9 \, dx\text{,}\) or \(dx = -{\textstyle\frac{1}{9}} \, du\text{,}\) and so

\begin{equation*} \int \frac{-18}{\left(14-9x\right)^{2}} \, dx = \int \frac{-18}{-9 u^{2}} \, du = 2 \int u^{-2} \, du. \end{equation*}

Integrating now gives

\begin{equation*} 2 \int u^{-2} \, du \textstyle =2\bigl( -\frac{1}{1} \bigr) u^{-1} + C = -2 u^{-1} + C. \end{equation*}

Therefore,

\begin{equation*} \int \frac{-18}{\left(14-9x\right)^{2}} \, dx = \textstyle -2\!\left(14-9x\right)^{-1}+C. \end{equation*}
