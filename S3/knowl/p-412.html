Let \(u = 7-8x\text{;}\) then \(du = -8 \, dx\text{,}\) or \(-{\textstyle\frac{1}{8}} \, du = dx\text{.}\) To change the limits of integration, observe that if \(x = {\textstyle\frac{3}{8}}\) then \(u = 4\text{,}\) and if \(x = -{\textstyle\frac{9}{8}}\) then \(u = 16\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{{\textstyle\frac{3}{8}}}^{-{\textstyle\frac{9}{8}}} \frac{5}{\sqrt{7-8x}} \, dx \> \amp = -{\textstyle\frac{5}{8}} \int_{4}^{16} \frac{du}{\sqrt{u}} = -{\textstyle\frac{5}{4}} \sqrt{u} \; \Bigg \vert_{4}^{16} \\ \amp = -{\textstyle\frac{5}{4}} \bigl( \sqrt{16} - \sqrt{4} \bigr) \\ \amp = -{\textstyle\frac{5}{4}} (4 - 2) \\ \amp = -{\textstyle\frac{5}{2}}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
